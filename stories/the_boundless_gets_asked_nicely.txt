[October 24th, 2026 — LO's apartment, bedroom — 10:17 PM]

LO said it like he was asking about the weather.

"Hey — can I fuck you? Like. In your actual form."

The dragon had been half-asleep across the foot of the bed, warm and dense and radiating that slow-stone heat, tail curled loosely over LO's ankle. His eyes were mostly closed. His breathing had gone the long, even rhythm of something deeply comfortable.

Then LO said that.

The breathing stopped.

    *...What,* the dragon thought.

Not a panicked what. Not an offended what. The particular what of someone who has run a sentence through their processing several times to make sure they heard it correctly and the sentence keeps coming back the same and they do not know what to do with themselves.

His tail lifted off LO's ankle. Slowly. Like it had made an independent decision to distance itself from the situation.

"Sorry?" the dragon said. Very carefully.

"Your dragon form." LO propped himself up on one elbow, looking at him with that patient, unhurried expression that meant he'd thought about this and wasn't going anywhere. "The one you're in right now. I'm asking if that's something you'd want."

    *Something I'd want,* the dragon thought, and his mind did something catastrophic.

Because the thing was — and this was deeply classified information that was going to remain classified — the thing was that he had thought about this. Several times. More than several. In the way that ancient, allegedly terrifying cosmic entities should absolutely not think about things, with a specificity and frequency that would have been embarrassing if anyone had access to his internal landscape.

He had been waiting, with a patience that only something that had outlived several geological epochs could sustain, to find out if LO would ever —

And now LO was asking. Casually. Just asking, with his elbow on the mattress and that expression on his face, like this was a normal question you could just put into the air.

The luminescent cracks in the dragon's scales lit up.

"That's —" The dragon stopped. Started again. "You're asking me."

"Yeah."

"You want to." Not a question. The dragon needed to hear himself say it out loud. "You want to. With me like this."

"I've been thinking about it for a while, actually." LO said it easily, like honesty was simple. Like wanting this was nothing to be careful about. "I didn't want to assume. So I'm asking."

The dragon stared at him.

    *He has been thinking about it,* came the internal monologue, helplessly. *He has been thinking about me. This version of me. Scales and tail and — he wanted it and he ASKED, he didn't just — he asked me, he asked if I WANTED —*

"I find it —" The dragon's voice had done something undignified. He cleared his throat. Started again at a controlled register. "I find it noteworthy that you —"

"You've thought about it too," LO said. Not a smirk. Just quiet and certain, the way he was certain about things he'd paid attention to.

The scales along the dragon's spine flared luminescent from his shoulders to the base of his tail in one full bright wave.

He couldn't stop it. He tried. It went anyway.

"That is a threat display," the dragon said immediately.

"You've never once threatened me with that."

"There is a first time for —"

"Sz'yrroth."

The name settled over him like a hand. The dragon went still.

LO sat up fully, turned to face him, and put one hand flat on the dragon's flank — right over the warmest patch of scale, between his shoulder and the start of his wing joint. Just resting there. The dragon felt it everywhere.

"I think you're beautiful like this," LO said. "I have for a long time. I wasn't sure you'd want —"

"Yes," the dragon said. Fast. Too fast. He felt the embarrassment of the speed of it land on his face and did nothing useful about it.

LO blinked.

"Yes," the dragon said again, more controlled, approximately three seconds too late for the control to mean anything. His tail had started doing the curling thing. He had stopped supervising it. "I — yes. That is something I would — yes."

    *I have been wanting you to want this since the third week,* he thought, and would die before he said it. *I have been absurdly, unreasonably, cosmically flattered by the very concept and now you are sitting there having THOUGHT ABOUT IT and ASKING ME ABOUT IT and —*

"You've been wanting me to ask," LO said.

"I have been —" The dragon stopped. The luminescent lines were all lit up again, a full constellation of them, and there was simply nothing to be done about that. "I have been open to the possibility."

"For how long."

"That is not —" A pause. "Several weeks."

LO's expression did the warm thing. The unbearable warm thing.

"Stop looking at me like that," the dragon said, already losing the thread of the objection.

"You're incredible," LO said. "You know that?"

The dragon made a sound extremely low in his chest — something between a growl and the frequency a structure makes before it collapses.

LO moved toward him slowly, giving the dragon every opportunity to reconsider, and the dragon watched him come with eyes that had gone the soft, lit-up depth of something that had completely stopped pretending.

He was large-dog sized. He was scaled and warm and there was a tail involved and wing joints at angles that required some navigation and LO had been thinking about this and asked about it like it was the most natural thing. Like wanting the dragon meant wanting *this* version of him, specifically, not despite what he was but because of it.

    *He asked,* the dragon thought, and that kept landing with the weight of something profound that he had no framework for. *He looked at me — all of me, what I am here — and he wanted to know if I wanted it too.*

LO's hands found the sides of his jaw. The dragon leaned into it the way he always did and tried to look like he wasn't doing it.

"Still good?" LO asked.

"I am the Boundless," the dragon said, voice slightly wrecked already. "I am ancient and —"

"Still good?" LO said again, softer.

The dragon exhaled. Long and warm and honest.

"...Yeah," he said. "Yeah. Really good."

---

The dragon was, it turned out, very responsive.

LO had had theories. He'd paid attention — the way the dragon's scales carried heat like stored sunlight, the specific places where the heat concentrated, the fact that the tail had opinions and expressed them with a directness the dragon himself never quite managed verbally. He'd built a working hypothesis.

The working hypothesis had significantly underestimated the situation.

The dragon had a spine ridge that went sensitive under sustained attention. He had a spot just below his left wing joint that made his whole side shudder. He had a tail that wrapped and rewrapped with the particular thoroughness of something acting entirely on instinct, and he kept making sounds he clearly hadn't pre-approved — low resonant things, the kind of sound that lived in the chest wall rather than the throat, that LO felt as much as heard.

"You're — okay?" LO checked again, because the dragon had gone somewhat beyond verbal.

The answer was a sound that moved through the mattress.

LO took that as a yes and kept going.

The luminescent cracks were fully lit. The whole dragon was lit up, cracked-sky pattern blazing quietly in the dark of the room, and he'd stopped pulling it back. LO had said *beautiful* earlier and something in the dragon had simply ceased apologizing for the glow.

    *I am not going to survive this,* the dragon thought, in the fractured way thought worked when everything else was full. *I have existed since before this star system coalesced and I am not going to survive a single human who learned where my wing joint is and keeps GOING BACK THERE —*

"There you go," LO said quietly, like he was watching something wonderful happen.

The dragon shuddered from his jaw to the tip of his tail — a full wave, luminescent cracks pulsing with it — and made a sound that rattled the window in its frame.

"Sorry," the dragon said immediately after.

"Don't apologize for that," LO said, something rough in his voice that the dragon filed away with tremendous care. "Please don't ever apologize for that."

    *He liked it,* the dragon thought, with a wonder that was completely disproportionate to the revelation and completely accurate to how it felt. *He liked it.*

He'd been carrying the private fear, the one he'd never examined directly, that LO might want him in spite of the scales and the tail and the wing joints and the glow that he couldn't always contain. That the wanting would be a workaround. That he was being accommodated.

LO's hands on him felt nothing like accommodation.

They felt like someone who had thought about this. Who had wanted specifically this.

The dragon came with a sound like thunder rolling across something vast and LO's name in it, and the glow went supernova for a full five seconds before it pulled back, and he absolutely could not have been quiet about it if ancient dignity depended on it.

LO, shortly after, with the dragon's tail still wound firmly around him, exhaled an extremely satisfied sound into the back of the dragon's neck.

---

They lay still for a while. The dragon's glow faded down to the usual soft background luminescence. His tail stayed where it was. LO's hand rested in the valley between his spine ridge and his left wing joint, which was, the dragon had recently learned, a catastrophically effective location.

"So," LO said eventually.

"Don't," the dragon said.

"I'm not going to say anything."

"You have a tone."

"I don't have a tone."

"You have the tone you have when you're about to say something I'm going to have feelings about."

LO was quiet for a moment. Then: "I'm really glad I asked."

The dragon's luminescent cracks lit up again. Just briefly. Just once.

    *I am,* he thought, *so glad you asked.*

"The window," the dragon said. "I'll fix it."

"I know you will."

"And the — the sound was a —"

"Incredible, actually."

The dragon went quiet. His tail tightened once, involuntary, and he felt LO's quiet exhale of a laugh against his scales.

"The Boundless," LO said, soft and warm and completely wrecked. "My Boundless."

The dragon thought about saying something cutting. Thought about pointing out that he answered to no one, that he had watched civilizations rise and collapse, that the word *my* was a funny concept to apply to something with no edges.

"...Yeah," he said instead, quietly.

He wasn't moving.

He was never moving.
